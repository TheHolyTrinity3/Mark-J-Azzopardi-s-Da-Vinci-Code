The Man Himself

Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo was described by Giorgio Vasari as a man whose "cast of mind was so heretical that he did not adhere to any religion, thinking perhaps that it was better to be a philosopher than a Christian." He taught himself Latin to read ancient texts in their original form. He questioned the nature of God, the Sun, the soul — and encoded those questions in his work.

LEONARDO DA VINCI — 15 LETTERS
LEONARDO DA VINCI → AN DECOLOR DIVINA
"Is the Divine Beyond Colour and Form?"

AN is the Latin question particle — is it that...? DECOLOR means colourless, beyond physical form, stripped of appearance. DIVINA means the divine. Hidden within his own name — the philosophical question that defined his life. A man who questioned whether God was beyond what the eye could see, encoding that question where no one would think to look.

Finding I
Mona Lisa
MONA LISA → ANIMA SOL
"Soul of the Sun"
The Mona Lisa is not a portrait of any living woman. She is the personification of the living Sun — Leonardo gave the Sun a human face. ANIMA means soul. SOL means the Sun. Every letter of the title accounts for this phrase exactly. She is the soul of the Sun made visible. He never gave the painting to the family who commissioned it. He kept her with him until he died.

Finding II
Virgin of the Rocks — Virgo in Rupibus
VIRGO IN RUPIBUS → SUB VIRGINI PURO
"Under the Pure Virgin"
Leonardo set this scene underground — inside the Earth, surrounded by rock and water. Every figure exists literally beneath the Virgin. SUB means under or beneath. VIRGINI means the Virgin. PURO means pure, clean, undefiled. The Virgin is the Earth herself — pure, untouched, the source of all life. Everything exists under her. We come from her and return to her.

Finding III
The Last Supper — Ultima Cena
ULTIMA CENA → ANIMA LUCET
"The Soul Shines"
ULTIMA CENA is the Latin title of The Last Supper. Every single letter rearranges perfectly into ANIMA LUCET — the soul shines. ANIMA means soul. LUCET means it shines, from LUCERE, to give light. Leonardo painted three windows behind Jesus with light streaming through deliberately. The soul shines. The divine light radiating from within — not from above, but from the soul itself.

Finding VI
Ginevra de' Benci
GINEVRA DE BENCI → VERDE BENIGNA CI
"A gracious green is here"
VERDE means green. BENIGNA means gracious, kind, benign. CI means here, present. The entire painting is dominated by the green juniper bush that frames Ginevra's face — juniper is ginepro in Italian, a deliberate pun on her name. Leonardo surrounded her with green virtue. The green is not decoration — it is her identity, her character, her soul made visible. Gracious and green, present before us. Leonardo is documented to have encoded anagrams in this painting's motto VIRTUTEM FORMA DECORAT — the encoding was his deliberate practice with this work.

Finding VIII
Dama con l'Ermellino — Lady with an Ermine
A portrait of Cecilia Gallerani, painted by Leonardo around 1489–1490 while he served Ludovico Sforza, Duke of Milan. She holds a white ermine — symbol of purity and the living Earth creature — against a blue dress representing the ocean, with a gold band across her forehead representing the Sun. The hand that holds the animal is the centrepiece — Leonardo painted every tendon, every knuckle, every fingernail with obsessive detail. The hand was the point. Through touch, through holding the Earth's creature, the soul is transmitted. This is energy transference made visible — the Sun above, the Earth in her arms, the ocean around her, and the human hand as the bridge between them.

The soul, the nourishing spirit, love, the hand, and the living creature — all hidden within a single title. Leonardo encoded the entire meaning of the portrait in its name.

Finding V
Salvator Mundi — Saviour of the World
SALVATOR MUNDI → VIS NATAM LUDOR
"Power is played with by what is born"
VIS means force or power. NATAM means the born one. LUDOR means I am played with, mocked — from LUDERE. Leonardo was mocked and criticised during his lifetime, called a bungler, considered heretical, forced to hide his most dangerous ideas in mirror script. The Saviour of the World, born into creation, whose power was played with and mocked. Leonardo encoded his own experience into the title of this painting. Every letter of SALVATOR MUNDI rearranges into this phrase exactly.

From His Own Notebooks
Leonardo's Reverence for the Sun and Earth

Leonardo never wrote a formal prayer — but his notebooks reveal a man who treated the Sun and Earth as living divine forces. In his own words:

"IL SOLE NO SI MUOVE" — The Sun does not move.

"Our body is dependent on heaven and heaven on the Spirit."

"The motive power is the cause of all life."

"The soul that dwells in that structure — for that indeed, be it what it may, is a thing divine."

"The Earth seeks to lose its life, desiring only continual reproduction."

He didn't pray to the Sun — he studied it with the devotion of someone who knew it was alive. He didn't worship the Earth — he described her as a living, breathing, desiring entity. Five hundred years before modern science confirmed it.

The Annunciation — NUNTIO Hidden Within

Within ANNUNTIATIO — the Latin word for the Annunciation — sits NUNTIO, meaning "I announce." The entire word is built around this core. Gabriel's act of announcing to Mary is encoded in the very title itself. The Annunciation is literally the act of divine message transmission — amplified announcement becoming event.

We come from her and return to her.

She is the human bridge between all three forces.

The hand is the centrepiece. Leonardo painted every tendon, every knuckle, every fingernail with extraordinary detail — more carefully than almost anything else in the painting. The hand holds the Earth's creature. Through that touch, soul energy transfers between the human and the living world.
