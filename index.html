The Foundation

The Method

Leonardo da Vinci was a known keeper of secrets. He wrote in mirror script, embedded geometry in his compositions, and encoded meaning across his life's work. The method applied here is precise and consistent across every finding.

I. Take the title of the painting as Leonardo named it.
II. Reduce to a unique letter pool — each letter represented once only.
III. Extract the strongest coherent Latin phrase from those letters.
IV. The meaning of the extracted phrase is evaluated against the painting's subject.

The strongest findings are those where the extracted phrase uses the letters economically and forms a complete, coherent Latin statement — leaving little or nothing to chance.

Visual Interpretations

Supporting Observations

The following observations are interpretive readings of the paintings themselves — presented honestly as symbolic analysis rather than constrained cipher findings.

Interpretation
The Vitruvian Man — Two States of Being

Two figures occupy the same body — one standing on the circle, one on the square. The conscious man stands grounded in the Universe. Behind him, arms wide, feet on the circle, is the subconscious — the soul of the Earth expressed through us. We are the Earth's living conscience. Our subconscious is her channel. We come from the Earth and we return to her.

Interpretation
Virgin of the Rocks — The Womb as Earth

Leonardo set this scene underground, inside the Earth, surrounded by rock and water. Every figure's gesture directs attention toward Mary's womb — the source, the origin, the place where Earth energy becomes life. The womb is the Earth's mechanism for expressing her soul through living form.

Interpretation
The Pointing Finger — A Recurring Signature

Across The Last Supper, Saint John the Baptist, Virgin of the Rocks, and the Annunciation — figures point upward. Not to an invisible God in the sky, but to the Sun. The source of all light, all energy, all life. Leonardo's consistent signature across his greatest works.
