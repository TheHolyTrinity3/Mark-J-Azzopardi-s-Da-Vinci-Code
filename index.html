The Man Himself

Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo was described by Giorgio Vasari as a man whose "cast of mind was so heretical that he did not adhere to any religion, thinking perhaps that it was better to be a philosopher than a Christian." He taught himself Latin to read ancient texts in their original form. He questioned the nature of God, the Sun, the soul — and encoded those questions in his work.

LEONARDO DA VINCI — 15 LETTERS
LEONARDO DA VINCI → AN DECOLOR DIVINA
"Is the Divine Beyond Colour and Form?"

AN is the Latin question particle — is it that...? DECOLOR means colourless, beyond physical form, stripped of appearance. DIVINA means the divine. Hidden within his own name — the philosophical question that defined his life. A man who questioned whether God was beyond what the eye could see, encoding that question where no one would think to look.

Finding I
Mona Lisa
MONA LISA → ANIMA SOL
"Soul of the Sun"
The Mona Lisa is not a portrait of any living woman. She is the personification of the living Sun — Leonardo gave the Sun a human face. ANIMA means soul. SOL means the Sun. Every letter of the title accounts for this phrase exactly. She is the soul of the Sun made visible. He never gave the painting to the family who commissioned it. He kept her with him until he died.

Finding II
The Last Supper — Ultima Cena
ULTIMA CENA → ANIMA LUCET
"The Soul Shines"
ULTIMA CENA is the Latin title of The Last Supper. Every single letter rearranges perfectly into ANIMA LUCET — the soul shines. ANIMA means soul. LUCET means it shines, from LUCERE, to give light. Leonardo painted three windows behind Jesus with light streaming through deliberately. The soul shines. The divine light radiating from within — not from above, but from the soul itself.

Visual Interpretations

Supporting Observations

The following are symbolic readings of the paintings — presented as interpretation rather than verified cipher findings.

Interpretation
The Vitruvian Man — Two States of Being

Two figures occupy the same body. One stands on the square — the Universe — conscious, structured, the man we know. Behind him, arms wide, feet on the circle, is the subconscious — the soul of the Earth expressed through us. We are the Earth's living conscience. Our subconscious is her channel. We come from her and return to her.

Interpretation
Virgin of the Rocks — The Womb as Earth

Leonardo set this scene underground — inside the Earth, surrounded by rock and water. Every figure's gesture directs attention toward the womb — the source, the origin of life. The womb is the Earth's mechanism for expressing her soul through living form.

Interpretation
Salvator Mundi — SOL Within the Saviour

SOL — the Sun — sits within SALVATOR MUNDI. The Sun-Man of the World. He holds the world in one hand and points upward to the source. The Sun above, the Earth below, man as the living bridge between them.

Interpretation
The Pointing Finger — A Recurring Signature

Across The Last Supper, Saint John the Baptist, Virgin of the Rocks, and the Annunciation — figures point upward. Not to an invisible God in doctrine, but to the Sun. The source of all light, all energy, all life. Leonardo's consistent signature encoded across his greatest works.
